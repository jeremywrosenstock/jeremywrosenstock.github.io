About

Jeremy Rosenstock is a composer and pianist based in Los Angeles, California. His principal teachers and mentors include Michael Pisaro, Wolfgang Von Schweinitz, Tim Feeney, Clay Chaplin, Stan Link, Michael Slayton, Michael Alec Rose, Craig Nies, and Gabriela Lena Frank. As a composer, Jeremy has taken lessons with Dan Trueman, Sarah Kirkland Snider, Andrea Mazzariello, Suzanne Farrin, Gabriela Lena Frank, David Conte, and Benjamin Boyle. He participated in the Gabriela Lena Frank Academy of Music’s Flute Masterclass (2021), where he worked with the namesake composer as well as flutist Nathalie Joachim, So Percussion Summer Institute (2019), where his quartet was read and recorded by So Percussion; Cortona Sessions for New Music (2017-2020), where his new work for soprano and violin was premiered by Duo Cortona in the summer of 2020; Stanford University’s CCRMA Summer Workshops (2017-2018, 2020), and the European-American Musical Alliance’s Nadia Boulanger Institute (2018).

As an undergraduate at Vanderbilt University’s Blair School of Music, Jeremy co-founded the Eschaton Ensemble, a student-run chamber group dedicated to the performance of new music. The group received an ensemble fellowship at the 2018 Cortona Sessions, premiering and performing numerous works by living composers (including his own) on piano and laptop. He was selected as a Semi-Finalist in 2019 for the Fulbright Program in Music Composition.

Jeremy received his MFA in Music Composition and Experimental Sound Practices from CalArts. His artistic interests include the integration of natural objects into chamber music, field recording, feedback systems, and just intonation. In the summer of 2021, he will be participating in Westben’s Virtual Performer-Composer Residency, International Contemporary Ensemble’s Ensemble Evolution, and Festival Mixtur’s Composition and Sound Experimentation Workshop.
